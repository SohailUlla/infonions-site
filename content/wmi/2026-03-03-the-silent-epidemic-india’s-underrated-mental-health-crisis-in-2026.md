OIL| March 2026

India in 2026 looks strong on paper. Growth is steady. Infrastructure is expanding. Global influence is rising.

But something quieter is happening beneath the headlines.

Young Indians are tired.

Not just “busy” tired  mentally exhausted. Academic competition is relentless. Workplaces are demanding. Social media fuels constant comparison. Add climate stress, rising costs of living, and uncertain futures and the pressure keeps building.

At the same time, millennials are carrying another weight: caring for ageing parents in a country that lacks structured eldercare support. Full-time jobs by day. Caregiving by night. Little emotional backup. Almost no systemic help.

This double strain youth burnout and caregiver fatigue  is becoming India’s silent epidemic.

Mental health remains underrated because it isn’t visible like traffic jams or pollution. But its impact is deeper. Burnout reduces productivity. Anxiety affects decision-making. Emotional fatigue weakens innovation.

A nation cannot run only on ambition. It runs on stable minds.

If India wants sustainable growth, mental health cannot remain a side conversation. It must move from stigma to policy priority in schools, workplaces, and public systems.

Because the true strength of a country is not just its GDP.

It is the resilience of its people.
